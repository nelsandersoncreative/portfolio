About

Hi, I’m Calvin Nels Anderson.    I’m a fullstack developer and tech enthusiast based in Southern California.

I am a recent graduate of the Front End Web Development bootcamp at Thinkful; I also hold a B.A. from the University of Southern California.  My passion for solving puzzles and conceptualizing ideas from inception to implementation led me to coding.  The idea of creating a transformative user experience with lines of code excites me.   I am passionate about the web, and love all the things that come along with programming: API's, functions, libraries and getting apps to do things gracefully and efficiently.   Staying productive whether that's on a team or working alone is one of my strong suits.   I always look forward to working with a great team of programmers and building new relationships.

When I’m not coding, I enjoy reading, hiking, playing music and cooking.  (On that note, have you ever had a maple apple gallette?)   My favorite subject is the next skill set I have to develop for a project.  Outside of spending my time coding, I'm learning to play the fiddle and hoping to write string quartets.

Cryptotracker

This app was built to provide users with a resource to create a digital wallet to which they can add their favorite cryptocurrencies and follow chronological price data for their chosen coins.  Cryptocurrency data is acquired via the CryptoCompare API and provides chronological price history back as far as 2010 to present-day.   Price data is viewable in days, weeks and months and includes line chart visualizations with clickable data points for specific dates.

National Park List

This app provides a clear and concise search of the U.S. National Park Service database. Users can search for parks as well as create a running list of parks they want to visit. National Park List uses National Park Service and Open Weather APIs to provide you with up-to-date information about all U.S. National parks, including current weather, available activities, map coordinates, park contact information and more. If you want to find a new and exciting National Park - National Park List is your plan.

Pop Chord Progressions

This app is designed to teach users of all backgrounds and skill levels to understand basic concepts relating to chord progressions in popular music. Furthermore, the user will be able to create their own chord progressions. Based on what chord progression(s) they create, the app will provide:

  • a playable recording of the user's chord progression
  • the user's chord progression in standard music notation
  • chord diagrams illustrating how to play the user's chord progression(s) on the piano
  • A list of songs the use the chosen chord progression(s)
